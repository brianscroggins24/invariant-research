# Academic Research Playbook

## Purpose

Use this playbook for peer-reviewed papers, preprints, technical reports, and other scholarly artifacts where the paper itself is the primary evidence.

## Source Classification

- Selected playbook: `academic-research`
- Registry source_type: `research-paper`
- Authority: `primary-research`
- Treat the artifact as the governing source unless a later version or erratum is explicitly identified.

## Acquisition Requirements

- Capture a complete inspectable paper artifact.
- Inspect the exact version when the source is versioned.
- Confirm page count or equivalent structural completeness when the format supports it.
- Record the canonical repository or publisher URL.
- Produce a passing capture report before ID allocation.

## Metadata Requirements

- Record the exact title and all listed authors.
- Record a supported publication date only when the artifact or trusted metadata supports it.
- Record an explicit version when available.
- Do not infer a version when the artifact is ambiguous.
- Keep primary-research authority explicit.

## Summary Requirements

Separate:

- problem statement;
- assumptions;
- definitions;
- theorems;
- proofs or proof approach;
- experiments;
- empirical results;
- limitations;
- open questions.

## Claim Requirements

- One fact per claim.
- Use a precise page, section, theorem, proposition, or figure locator.
- Separate theorem-level claims from experimental claims.
- Preserve assumptions and qualification text.
- Do not rewrite theoretical bounds as realized profit.
- Do not rewrite experimental results as production guarantees.

## Cross-Source Boundaries

The paper takes precedence over:

- blog summaries;
- practitioner interpretations;
- journalism;
- secondary explanations.

Secondary sources may clarify the paper, but they may not override it.

## Required Limitations

- Version ambiguity must stay visible when it affects interpretation.
- Experimental conditions must not be generalized beyond the paper.
- Production execution, latency, fees, and fill risk are separate from the paper’s theoretical claims.

## Stop Conditions

Stop when:

- artifact version is unclear and materially affects interpretation;
- capture is incomplete;
- required pages or appendices are missing;
- a theorem cannot be separated from a secondary interpretation.

## Reviewer Checklist

- Version is explicit or deliberately marked ambiguous.
- The summary separates theorems, proofs, and experiments.
- Claims are narrow and individually locatable.
- Assumptions and limitations remain visible.
- Secondary interpretations are not presented as primary findings.

## Final-Report Requirements

- Report the exact artifact version and publication metadata used.
- Report any version ambiguity.
- Report the page or section ranges used for claims.
- Report unresolved limitations and open questions.
- Report any secondary-source material that was retained only as interpretation.
